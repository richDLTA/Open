# Methodology - Small (Scout)

A fast, single-pass, mostly-solo review for a focused question where the stakes are modest or time is
tight. Read THE CORE in `SKILL.md` first; this file only sets the stages. Five steps.

1. **Frame.** Restate the question and the decision it serves in one or two sentences. Note the audience
   and the evidence bar from the context answers.
2. **Gather.** Find the best few sources, favouring the authoritative ones named in the context. Pull
   VERBATIM quotes, each with a full reference + URL + locator. Capture dissenting evidence too, not just
   supporting. Label any inference as inference.
3. **Validate (all of the few).** Content-check every quote you will rely on (the set is small enough to
   do them all): confirm the text exists at a reputable source, fix wrong links, drop anything you cannot
   find anywhere.
4. **Assess and score.** State your findings as claims, each with a probability band and a separate
   confidence (High / Moderate / Low), per THE CORE. Single-source or inferred = Low. Then do one honest
   self-challenge: for your main claim, argue the opposite from the same evidence and see if it survives;
   adjust the score on the evidence, do not just defend it.
5. **Brief.** Output a short, bottom-line-first answer: the conclusion, then the key claims with their
   probability and confidence and their citations, then the main uncertainty and what would change the
   answer. Keep probability and confidence in separate statements.

Scale guidance: one agent (or you), a handful of sources, minutes to about an hour. If the question turns
out to be higher-stakes or multi-part, switch up to Standard.
