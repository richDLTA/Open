# Methodology - Large (Programme)

The full programme for high-stakes, cross-domain, or foundational work where being wrong is expensive.
Read THE CORE in `SKILL.md` first. Nine stages. Tier markings (FAST / CAPABLE / DEEP / APEX) are per THE
CORE; APEX (e.g. Fable-class) appears ONLY at the judgement nodes marked, and always reads compressed
artefacts (the ledger, the manifest, the challenge log), never raw browsing transcripts.

0. **Preparation. [APEX plans]** Complexity pre-check. APEX writes the decomposition, the source taxonomy
   (source types against information types) with a mandatory adversarial or critical category, and
   PER-AGENT BUDGETS (tool calls and tokens) so effort scales with the stakes, not curiosity. FAST
   populates sources widely (a fast tier for breadth, a frontier tier for precision and adversarial
   sources); CAPABLE deduplicates, sweeps for gaps, and assembles a graded brief. Grade every source for
   RELIABILITY (Admiralty A-F + a credibility grade). If the consumer also needs RELEVANCE to their
   estate or domain, track that as a SEPARATE metric, not part of reliability.
1. **Evidence gathering. [FAST/CAPABLE]** Agents collect TYPED evidence per THE CORE (verbatim quotes
   with full reference + URL + locator; primary-data, code, and official-record artefacts by their own
   methods); ARCHIVE AT CAPTURE; reliability recorded per item; inference labelled as inference.
   Everything is written to the ledger incrementally, with collection gaps noted as they appear.
2. **Validation gate. [CAPABLE, fresh]** A FRESH agent validates EVERY item content-first against the
   capture-time snapshot; graded verdict; a fabricated item is stripped and flags the claim it supported.
   Analysis is not trusted until this passes.
3. **Analysis and scoring. [DEEP drafts, APEX scores]** Build claims from validated evidence. DEEP drafts
   per sub-area; APEX is the scorer of record, setting every probability band and separate confidence
   from the rubric over the assembled ledger; conservative priors; the two axes never in one sentence.
4. **Adversarial challenge. [APEX]** Per Challenge integrity in THE CORE: a fresh, blinded APEX
   challenger must EVIDENCE every objection to the same bar; the ORIGINAL author defends on evidence,
   concedes only when out-evidenced, and holds otherwise; pressure and authority carry zero weight; a
   claim that survives a strong challenge may legitimately gain confidence. Bounded to one challenge +
   one rebuttal + adjudication; APEX adjudicates and certifies the challenge was substantive.
5. **Customer-acceptance review. [CAPABLE/DEEP]** Agents embody the END CONSUMERS of the research and ask
   "would this satisfy me, what would I ask, what would block my decision?" Inquisitive and representing
   the consumer's needs, not a fault-hunt (the factual fault-finding already happened in Stages 2 and 4).
   If the output feeds another tool, include a MACHINE consumer: is the schema complete, is every claim
   unambiguous, is there orphan evidence?
6. **Synthesis and adjudication. [APEX]** One APEX arbiter reads the validated ledger, the challenge
   exchanges, and the consumer feedback; adjudicates each contested claim on evidence; sets the FINAL
   scores; records unresolved disagreement as conflict-as-data; completes the run manifest; and produces
   the unified, phased output.
7. **Implementation handoff.** If the output drives action, hand the phased plan to implementers (quick
   wins, then moderate, then advanced). Phased plans drive far higher follow-through than unphased ones.
8. **Knowledge integration and retrieval-first.** Add the VALIDATED ledger to a durable knowledge base
   or graph so it compounds, and READ THAT FIRST on future questions before going external. Review-by
   dates trigger refresh; track whether "highly likely" judgements come true and feed the calibration
   back (Tetlock).

Scale guidance: roughly seven to fourteen-plus agents in waves, hundreds of sources, one to a few days.
Spend most tokens in FAST and CAPABLE; APEX should be a handful of dense passes at the marked gates, not
a resident orchestrator. This is deliberately heavy; do not use it for routine questions (use Scout or
Standard).
