# Methodology - Large (Programme)

The full programme for high-stakes, cross-domain, or foundational work where being wrong is expensive.
Read THE CORE in `SKILL.md` first. Nine stages.

0. **Preparation.** Complexity pre-check; build a source taxonomy (source types against information types)
   with a mandatory adversarial or critical category; populate sources widely (a fast tier for breadth, a
   frontier tier for precision and adversarial sources); deduplicate; sweep for gaps; assemble a graded
   brief. Grade every source for RELIABILITY (Admiralty A-F + a credibility grade). If the consumer also
   needs RELEVANCE to their estate or domain, track that as a SEPARATE metric, not part of reliability.
1. **Evidence gathering.** Agents collect VERBATIM quotes, each with a full reference + URL + locator;
   reliability recorded per quote; inference labelled as inference.
2. **Validation gate.** A FRESH agent validates EVERY quote content-first; graded verdict; a fabricated
   quote is stripped and flags the claim it supported. Analysis is not trusted until this passes.
3. **Analysis and scoring.** Build claims from validated evidence, each with a probability band and a
   separate confidence from the rubric; conservative priors; the two axes never in one sentence.
4. **Adversarial challenge.** A challenger must EVIDENCE every objection to the same bar; the ORIGINAL
   author defends on evidence, concedes only when out-evidenced, and holds otherwise; pressure and
   authority carry zero weight; a claim that survives a strong challenge may legitimately gain confidence.
   Bounded to one challenge + one rebuttal + adjudication.
5. **Customer-acceptance review.** Agents embody the END CONSUMERS of the research and ask "would this
   satisfy me, what would I ask, what would block my decision?" Inquisitive and representing the
   consumer's needs, not a fault-hunt (the factual fault-finding already happened in Stages 2 and 4).
6. **Synthesis and adjudication.** One arbiter reads the validated research, the challenge exchanges, and
   the consumer feedback; adjudicates each contested claim on evidence; sets the FINAL scores; records
   unresolved disagreement as conflict-as-data; and produces the unified, phased output.
7. **Implementation handoff.** If the output drives action, hand the phased plan to implementers (quick
   wins, then moderate, then advanced). Phased plans drive far higher follow-through than unphased ones.
8. **Knowledge integration and retrieval-first.** Add the VALIDATED research to a durable knowledge base
   or graph so it compounds, and READ THAT FIRST on future questions before going external. Track whether
   "highly likely" judgements come true and feed the calibration back (Tetlock).

Scale guidance: roughly seven to fourteen-plus agents in waves, hundreds of sources, one to a few days.
This is deliberately heavy; do not use it for routine questions (use Scout or Standard).
