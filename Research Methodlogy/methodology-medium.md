# Methodology - Medium (Standard)

The balanced default for most real decisions: a small team of agents, a graded source base, and real
validation and challenge steps. Read THE CORE in `SKILL.md` first. Six stages.

0. **Prepare.** Map the source landscape for the question, including a deliberate ADVERSARIAL or critical
   category (sources likely to disagree). Grade each source for RELIABILITY (for example the Admiralty
   scale: source reliability A-F plus an information-credibility grade). Assemble a short brief framed as
   "starting sources, not a ceiling".
1. **Gather.** One agent per sub-area collects VERBATIM quotes, each with a full reference + URL + locator,
   recording the source's reliability. Inference is labelled as inference, never dressed as a quote.
2. **Validate gate.** A FRESH agent validates EVERY quote content-first (search the text, not just the
   link), using the graded verdict in THE CORE. Nothing proceeds to analysis on an unverified quote.
3. **Analyse and score.** Turn validated evidence into claims, each with a probability band and a separate
   confidence from the rubric. Keep the two in separate sentences. Apply conservative priors against
   overconfidence.
4. **Challenge (one round).** A challenger raises EVIDENCED objections, held to the same quote-and-
   reference bar as the author. The original author defends with evidence and concedes only where it is
   out-evidenced; tone, repetition, and pressure carry no weight. Move each score by the strength of the
   evidence, not by who pushed hardest.
5. **Synthesise.** Reconcile across agents, set the FINAL probability and confidence per claim, record any
   unresolved disagreement as data (both sides, with their evidence, no forced winner), and produce the
   output the context asked for (a brief, a memo, or a phased plan).

Scale guidance: about three to seven agents, tens of sources, hours. If the audience needs convincing or
the findings will be reused, add the customer-acceptance review and the knowledge-integration steps from
the Programme file.
